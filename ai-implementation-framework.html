AI Governance · Runtime Implementation

AI Implementation Framework

A practical implementation model for converting AI governance principles into runtime controls across users, applications, agents, documents, models, vendors, and production environments.

Why this framework exists

The Agentic AI Governance Framework defines how autonomous AI should be governed. The AI Implementation Framework explains how those governance controls are enforced in real systems: from user intent and prompt normalization to document control, vendor routing, evidence gates, validation, human approval, and immutable audit.

Core principle: Production AI execution is denied by default and allowed only when the business scenario, evidence pack, prompt version, policy version, model/vendor route, document controls, and runtime safeguards are approved.
Enterprise AI Control Plane — High-Level Implementation Flow

Implementation aspects

1 · Entry

Identity & Environment Resolver

Every user, application, API, or agent is authenticated first. The gateway resolves environment policy: development, testing/UAT, or production.

2 · Prompt

Business Intent & Prompt Compiler

Users can ask in natural language. The platform interprets intent, detects task type, enriches the prompt, and creates a structured AI request.

3 · Scenario

Business Scenario Registry

Every AI request must map to an approved business scenario with owner, risk level, data classification, allowed actions, required tests, and status.

4 · Documents

Document Control Layer

Documents are treated as governed assets. PDFs, Office files, images, emails, and spreadsheets are classified, permission-checked, masked, chunked, and tracked.

5 · Policy

Policy Decision Engine

The engine decides whether to allow, allow with controls, require approval, or block based on scenario, data, user, model, vendor, and environment.

6 · Security

Filter · Mask · Classify

PII, PHI, PCI, secrets, prompt injection, content safety, data sensitivity, and output leakage checks are applied before and after AI execution.

7 · Control Packs

Scenario Control Adapters

Communication, knowledge, document, decision, calculation, workflow, compliance, developer, and agentic use cases get different controls and tests.

8 · Gate

Evidence & Release Gate

Production is blocked unless the scenario, prompt, model/vendor route, policy version, security review, test reports, and approvals are complete.

9 · Routing

Approved AI Routing

Requests route only to approved external providers, managed enterprise platforms, self-hosted models, private pipelines, or third-party AI platforms.

10 · Output

Validation & Rehydration

Responses are schema-checked, confidence-scored, source-validated, scanned for leakage, and rehydrated only when approved.

11 · Oversight

Human-in-the-Loop

High-risk outputs, business decisions, customer-impacting communication, and agentic actions are routed to human review before final delivery.

12 · Evidence

Immutable Audit Vault

The platform stores request, response, scenario, data class, document ID, prompt version, model route, approvals, cost, and final business action.

Content re-embedding for RAG and knowledge scenarios

When documents are used for knowledge retrieval, re-embedding is controlled as a governed data movement event. The goal is to keep retrieval accurate, permission-aware, and auditable.

How this ties to the Agentic AI Governance Framework

The Agentic AI Governance Framework defines how autonomous systems must be contained. This implementation framework shows where those controls live in the architecture.

Agentic governance conceptImplementation control
Blast radiusTransaction caps, rate limits, bounded actions, automatic suspension, kill switch.
BoundariesSandboxed execution, data route enforcement, approved tool/API allowlists.
PermissionsLeast-privilege service accounts, role-based access, tool-level authorization.
Segregation of dutiesAgent can prepare or initiate, but cannot approve its own final action.
Human reviewRisk-based HITL routing before customer-impacting or system-changing actions.
Immutable logsFull session trace, tool calls, approvals, outputs, costs, and evidence retained.

Vendor and model assurance

Third-party AI routes are not approved only because a vendor says data is not stored or used for training. The route must carry evidence: contract terms, retention and deletion commitments, backup expiry, no-training assurance, sub-processor review, audit reports, and customer-specific model isolation where training or fine-tuning is allowed.
