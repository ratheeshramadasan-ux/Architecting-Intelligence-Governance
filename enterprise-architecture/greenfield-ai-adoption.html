Greenfield AI Adoption & Governance Framework

A practical framework for organizations beginning their AI journey without an established AI operating model, governance structure, adoption pathway, decision-rights model, or production control environment.

Maturity-sensitiveRisk-proportionateAdoption-ledGovernance-by-designImplementation-ready
01 · Starting Point

Greenfield adoption is not only a technology program

A greenfield client may have limited AI literacy, fragmented data ownership, unclear decision rights, inconsistent risk processes, shadow AI usage, and no shared definition of what “ready for AI” means. The framework therefore builds the organizational foundation and the AI capability together.

Core design principle
Governance intensity, operating-model complexity and control requirements must be calibrated to three variables: organizational maturity, AI capability maturity, and the risk and impact of the proposed use case.
Dimension 1

Enterprise maturity

Leadership alignment, process discipline, architecture governance, risk management, change capability, data ownership and operational controls.

Dimension 2

AI maturity

AI literacy, use-case portfolio, data and model capability, engineering practices, monitoring, assurance, product ownership and production support.

Dimension 3

Use-case risk

Impact on people, financial decisions, regulated outcomes, privacy, safety, autonomy, external exposure and reversibility of errors.

02 · Adoption Journey

Progressive adoption from awareness to AI-native operations

The organization advances only when defined entry criteria, controls, capabilities and evidence are in place. This prevents premature scaling and avoids applying enterprise-grade overhead to low-risk learning use cases.

STAGE 01

AI Awareness

Leadership alignment, workforce literacy, acceptable-use policy, AI inventory and shadow AI discovery.

Exit: baseline policy + accountable sponsor
STAGE 02

Assisted Productivity

Approved copilots, low-risk use cases, human review, data restrictions and adoption measurement.

Exit: controlled usage + value evidence
STAGE 03

Controlled Experimentation

Sandbox, intake process, risk classification, pilot standards, technical review and documented learning.

Exit: repeatable pilot governance
STAGE 04

Operational AI

Production gates, service ownership, monitoring, incident response, support model and lifecycle accountability.

Exit: stable production controls
STAGE 05

Enterprise Scaling

Reusable platforms, federated governance, portfolio funding, common controls and cross-business adoption.

Exit: portfolio-level governance
STAGE 06

AI-Native Transformation

Process redesign, agentic workflows, advanced assurance, autonomous decision support and continuous optimization.

Exit: adaptive enterprise capability
03 · Foundation Assessment

The first consulting deliverable is not a generic policy or RACI. It is a fact-based baseline showing what already exists, what can be extended, and what must be created from the ground up.

  • Priority business capabilities
  • Risk appetite and funding model
  • Executive sponsorship
Data & Technology

Readiness landscape

  • Data quality and ownership
  • Cloud, integration and identity
  • Model and vendor landscape
  • Security and observability
Governance

Control baseline

  • Existing committees and policies
  • Decision rights and escalation
  • Privacy, legal and risk review
  • Audit and evidence practices
People

Adoption capacity

  • AI literacy and skills
  • Change absorption
  • Role and workforce impact
  • Trust and communication
04 · Target Operating Model

Build the organizational machinery that makes AI governable

The operating model separates enterprise policy ownership, business accountability, delivery responsibility, independent challenge and production operations. A centralized, federated or hybrid structure is selected based on maturity, scale and regulatory exposure.

Executive Layer

AI Steering Committee

Sets ambition, approves risk appetite and funding, resolves material conflicts, accepts major residual risk and sponsors enterprise adoption.

Governance Layer

AI Governance Council

Owns policy, risk tiers, exception rules, lifecycle requirements, control standards, portfolio oversight and material approvals.

Delivery Layer

AI Working Group

Coordinates business, data, architecture, engineering, legal, privacy, security, risk, change and operations for active initiatives.

Assurance Layer

Independent Review

Provides challenge through model risk, compliance, privacy, security, internal audit and specialist assurance where required.

Platform Layer

AI Enablement Function

Provides approved platforms, reusable patterns, prompt and model controls, observability, engineering standards and technical guardrails.

Business Layer

Use-Case Ownership

Owns the problem, expected value, process integration, user adoption, human oversight, outcome quality and residual business risk.

RACI positioning
The RACI is one artifact within the operating model. It must be supported by governance charters, decision-rights matrices, committee mandates, escalation thresholds, approval gates, exception handling, evidence requirements and stop-use authority.
05 · Governed Use-Case Lifecycle

Translate governance into decision gates across the full lifecycle

Every stage identifies the accountable owner, required evidence, control obligations, approval authority, escalation trigger and go/no-go criteria.

01

Ideation & Intake

Problem definition, sponsor, intended users, affected process, data needs and preliminary value.

02

Risk Classification

Impact, autonomy, sensitivity, regulation, external exposure, reversibility and human oversight.

03

Feasibility & Prioritization

Data readiness, technical fit, cost, value, adoption complexity, dependencies and control burden.

04

Design

Architecture, model choice, data flow, security, privacy, user experience and operating procedures.

05

Build & Configure

Engineering standards, prompt controls, retrieval, integrations, access boundaries and audit logging.

06

Validate

Accuracy, robustness, bias, privacy, security, explainability, human factors and failure modes.

07

Approve & Deploy

Evidence review, residual risk acceptance, operational readiness, rollback and communications.

08

Operate & Monitor

Performance, adoption, drift, incidents, exceptions, vendor changes and control effectiveness.

09

Change & Reapprove

Material model, prompt, data, integration, autonomy or regulatory changes trigger reassessment.

10

Retire

Decommissioning, data disposition, access removal, records retention and successor transition.

06 · Control Domains

Apply proportionate controls without slowing every use case equally

Data

Data & privacy

Classification, minimization, consent, residency, retention, lineage, grounding data approval and PII handling.

Model

Model assurance

Qualification, limitations, evaluation, hallucination testing, bias, explainability, version control and drift.

Security

Cyber & identity

Access control, prompt injection defence, secret handling, tool permissions, segregation of duties and logging.

Human

Oversight & safety

Human-in-the-loop rules, override, appeal, stop-use authority, user disclosure and high-impact decision review.

Vendor

Third-party AI

Training restrictions, retention, subcontractors, audit rights, model changes, breach duties and exit strategy.

Operations

Runtime controls

Monitoring, thresholds, fallback, rollback, incident response, resilience, support ownership and kill switch.

Legal

Legal & IP

Copyright, licensing, contractual use, output ownership, regulatory obligations, records and disclosure.

Evidence

Auditability

Decision records, test evidence, approvals, exceptions, model lineage, prompt versions and immutable logs.

08 · People & Adoption

Build organizational confidence and capability alongside controls

Leadership

Common ambition, sponsorship, visible accountability, funding decisions and role modelling.

AI Literacy

Role-based education for executives, users, builders, reviewers, risk functions and operations.

Change & Trust

Stakeholder mapping, communication, concerns, transparency, workforce impact and feedback.

Role Redesign

Human oversight, new accountabilities, revised procedures, skills pathways and support structures.

Adoption Analytics

Usage, satisfaction, proficiency, value realization, failure demand, workarounds and control adherence.

09 · Implementation Roadmap

Build the foundation, prove it through pilots, then scale

The exact timing depends on the client’s starting point, but the work should proceed in controlled waves with tangible deliverables and decision gates.

PHASE 1 · DISCOVER

Baseline & Align

  • AI ambition and value thesis
  • Maturity and landscape assessment
  • Shadow AI inventory
  • Stakeholder and risk mapping
PHASE 2 · ESTABLISH

Governance Foundation

  • Operating model and charters
  • Decision rights and RACI
  • Risk tiers and lifecycle gates
  • Policies and minimum controls
PHASE 3 · ENABLE & PILOT

Controlled Adoption

  • Approved sandbox and platforms
  • Priority pilot portfolio
  • Training and change plan
  • Evidence-based go/no-go reviews
PHASE 4 · OPERATE & SCALE

Enterprise Capability

  • Production support and monitoring
  • Federated governance
  • Reusable patterns and controls
  • Portfolio value and maturity reviews
10 · Success Measures

Measure governance as an enabler of trusted value

Value

Business outcomes

Revenue, productivity, cycle time, service quality, cost avoidance, risk reduction and user outcomes.

Adoption

Capability uptake

Active usage, proficiency, satisfaction, repeat use, process penetration and workforce confidence.

Governance

Control performance

Time to decision, exceptions, incidents, test failures, audit findings, control effectiveness and remediation.

Framework outcome
The target is not maximum governance. The target is the minimum sufficient governance required to create trust, preserve accountability, reduce material risk and allow valuable AI use cases to move safely from idea to enterprise scale.
