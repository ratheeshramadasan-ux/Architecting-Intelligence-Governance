Greenfield AI Adoption & Governance Framework

A practical stage-wise journey that helps business, technology, security and governance teams introduce AI together without losing human accountability, trust or operational clarity.

AI supports people. People remain accountable for important decisions, outcomes and trust.
Visual overview

From first conversation to enterprise-scale AI

Each stage answers one simple human question and separates the controls needed to start safely from the controls used for scaled, regulated or agentic AI.

🎯01
Stage 01

Establish AI Direction

What are we trying to improve?

Agree the vision, sponsor, outcomes, stakeholders and guiding principles.

Minimum required

Named sponsor, business owner, purpose and stakeholder list.

Standard enterprise

Strategy, portfolio roadmap, funding and benefits measurement.

Leading practice

Board-level direction and enterprise value governance.

🧭02
Stage 02

Understand the Starting Environment

What can we build upon?

Assess processes, RPA, data, applications, security, infrastructure and workforce readiness.

Minimum required

Process, technology, data and security inventories.

Standard enterprise

Readiness assessment, dependencies and capability heat map.

Leading practice

Lineage, process mining and shadow-AI discovery.

⚖️03
Stage 03

Define Risk Appetite

Where should people remain in control?

Define where AI may assist, advise or act and who may accept residual risk.

Minimum required

Permitted uses, prohibited uses, human review and escalation.

Standard enterprise

Risk tiers, autonomy limits and formal acceptance authority.

Leading practice

Dynamic thresholds and portfolio-level appetite monitoring.

🗂️04
Stage 04

Classify Data for AI Use

What information is appropriate to use?

Classify public, internal, confidential and highly restricted information and assign an approved AI treatment.

Minimum required

Data owner, class, access rule, retention rule and sensitive-data restriction.

Standard enterprise

Masking, tokenization, approved repositories and embedding lifecycle.

Leading practice

Automated classification, dynamic masking, lineage and policy routing.

🛡️05
Stage 05

Define Security & Infrastructure

How do we protect people and the organization?

Set identity, access, encryption, environment, network, logging, monitoring and incident requirements.

Minimum required

Authentication, RBAC, encryption, environment separation, logs and shutdown.

Standard enterprise

Private connectivity, DLP, secrets management and security testing.

Leading practice

AI gateway, runtime policy enforcement and automated containment.

🔎06
Stage 06

Select & Qualify AI Products

Which tool genuinely fits our needs?

Evaluate business fit, security, data handling, integration, governance, vendor terms, cost and exit options.

Minimum required

Controlled evaluation, security review and approval record.

Standard enterprise

Qualification scorecard, architecture review and vendor assurance.

Leading practice

Benchmark suites, continuous vendor monitoring and model routing.

🏛️07
Stage 07

Establish the Operating Model

Who makes decisions and supports the teams?

Create executive, governance, delivery, operations and assurance layers.

Minimum required

Named owners and an agreed approval route.

Standard enterprise

Governance charter, forums, mandates and decision rights.

Leading practice

Federated governance and a formal AI management system.

🤝08
Stage 08

Define RACI & Stakeholder Acceptance

Does everyone understand their role?

Clarify who performs, owns, advises and receives updates, then capture formal acceptance.

Minimum required

Lifecycle RACI, communication matrix, acceptance register and escalation path.

Standard enterprise

Stage-gate acceptance across business, security, IT and governance.

Leading practice

Automated workflow, dashboards and periodic role reassessment.

🏗️09
Stage 09

Build the AI Foundation

Are the right foundations ready?

Prepare identity, approved models, document controls, masking, re-embedding, evidence, testing, monitoring and training.

Minimum required

Approved endpoints, access checks, masking, logging, testing and human approval.

Standard enterprise

Scenario registry, document controls, automated testing and evidence gates.

Leading practice

Control plane, deny-by-default routing, prompt defence and continuous evaluation.

🧪10
Stage 10

Implement Controlled Pilots

Does this make work better for users?

Select manageable use cases, test value and controls, gather feedback and confirm human oversight.

Minimum required

Approved use case, owner, risk class, tests and acceptance criteria.

Standard enterprise

Evaluation plan, stakeholder feedback and documented pilot evidence.

Leading practice

Red teaming, synthetic tests, bias evaluation and continuous telemetry.

🚀11
Stage 11

Move to Production

Are people ready and supported?

Confirm business, security, data, operational, governance and support readiness.

Minimum required

Monitoring, support, rollback, acceptance and audit evidence.

Standard enterprise

Formal readiness checklist and residual-risk approval.

Leading practice

Automated release gates, canary deployment and resilience testing.

📈12
Stage 12

Monitor, Manage & Improve

Is the AI still behaving as expected?

Track value, quality, security, privacy, operations, incidents, controls and material changes.

Minimum required

Thresholds, owners, incident route, evidence and periodic review.

Standard enterprise

Business, AI, security and operational dashboards.

Leading practice

Continuous evaluation, drift detection and automated control testing.

🌐13
Stage 13

Scale & Industrialize

How do we expand without losing trust?

Reuse platforms, patterns and controls while progressively increasing scale and autonomy.

Minimum required

Proven production pattern, portfolio owner and reusable controls.

Standard enterprise

Shared services, portfolio governance and maturity roadmap.

Leading practice

AI product factory, federated governance and continuous assurance.
