Greenfield AI Adoption & Governance

A human-centred, stage-wise framework for organizations introducing AI without an established operating model, governance structure or production control environment.

Human principle: AI supports people. People remain accountable for important decisions, outcomes and trust.

Thirteen stages from direction to scale

1. Establish AI Direction

Vision, sponsor, outcomes, principles and stakeholders.

2. Understand the Starting Environment

Processes, data, technology, security, automation and workforce readiness.

3. Define Risk Appetite

Where AI may assist, advise or act and who can accept residual risk.

4. Classify Data for AI Use

Public, internal, confidential and restricted information with approved treatments.

5. Define Security & Infrastructure

Identity, access, encryption, environments, network, logging and incident controls.

6. Select & Qualify AI Products

Business fit, security, data handling, integration, cost, terms and exit.

7. Establish the Operating Model

Executive, governance, delivery, operations and assurance layers.

8. Define RACI & Acceptance

Accountability, consultation, escalation and formal stakeholder acceptance.

9. Build the AI Foundation

Approved models, identity, data controls, testing, evidence and monitoring.

10. Implement Controlled Pilots

Low-risk, bounded use cases with representative users and measurable outcomes.

11. Move to Production

Business, architecture, security, privacy, legal and operational gates.

12. Monitor, Manage & Improve

Value, quality, safety, access, cost, drift, incidents and user feedback.

13. Scale & Industrialize

Reusable patterns, shared platforms, assurance and continuous improvement.

Minimum foundation before production

Governance

Named sponsor, business owner, AI owner, risk tier, RACI, approvals and escalation.

Technology

Identity, approved models, data classification, DLP, logging, monitoring and rollback.

People and Adoption

Training, acceptable use, communication, support model, human review and feedback channels.
