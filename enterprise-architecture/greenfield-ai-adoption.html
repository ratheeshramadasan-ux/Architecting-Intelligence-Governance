Greenfield AI Adoption & Governance Framework

A detailed, human-centred adoption model for organizations introducing AI without an established operating model, governance structure, technical foundation or production control environment.

Visual overview

Thirteen stages from direction to enterprise scale

Each stage defines the business question, required activities, expected deliverables, minimum controls, standard enterprise controls and leading-practice capabilities.

🎯
Stage 01

Establish AI Direction

What are we trying to improve, and why does AI matter?

Translate business strategy into a clear AI ambition, measurable outcomes and accountable sponsorship.

Minimum controls

  • Named executive sponsor and business owner
  • Defined purpose and intended users
  • Initial stakeholder map
  • Explicit human-accountability principle

Enterprise controls

  • AI strategy and investment roadmap
  • Benefits, cost and risk measures
  • Portfolio governance and funding model
  • Alignment with enterprise strategy

Leading practice

  • Board-approved AI ambition
  • Enterprise value and trust scorecard
  • Scenario-based strategic planning
  • Continuous portfolio optimization
Deliverables: AI vision, strategic principles, sponsor mandate, outcome map, initial roadmap and stakeholder register.
🧭
Stage 02

Understand the Starting Environment

What capabilities, constraints and dependencies already exist?

Assess business processes, data, applications, automation, infrastructure, security, skills and organizational readiness.

Minimum controls

  • Current-state inventory
  • Critical process and data identification
  • Known technical and regulatory constraints
  • Skills and ownership gaps

Enterprise controls

  • Formal AI maturity assessment
  • Capability heatmap
  • Architecture and integration baseline
  • Readiness remediation plan

Leading practice

  • Quantified maturity benchmarks
  • Automated dependency discovery
  • Workforce and change analytics
  • Continuous maturity tracking
Deliverables: maturity assessment, capability map, dependency inventory, readiness score and prioritized gap-remediation plan.
⚖️
Stage 03

Define Risk Appetite

Where may AI assist, advise or act, and where must people remain in control?

Set acceptable uses, prohibited uses, autonomy boundaries, risk acceptance authorities and escalation requirements.

Minimum controls

  • Permitted and prohibited use statements
  • Human-review requirements
  • Named residual-risk owner
  • Basic impact and reversibility test

Enterprise controls

  • Risk-tier methodology
  • Autonomy and impact thresholds
  • Formal risk acceptance route
  • Control requirements by tier

Leading practice

  • Dynamic risk scoring
  • Policy-as-code enforcement
  • Real-time autonomy restriction
  • Continuous risk reclassification
Deliverables: AI risk appetite, prohibited-use register, autonomy model, escalation matrix and risk acceptance authority.
🗂️
Stage 04

Classify Data for AI Use

What information may AI access, transform, retain or expose?

Map enterprise data classes to approved AI treatments, residency rules, retention, masking and access requirements.

Minimum controls

  • Public, internal, confidential and restricted classes
  • Approved and blocked data treatments
  • Basic PII and sensitive-data detection
  • Source ownership

Enterprise controls

  • DLP and classification integration
  • Lineage and consent controls
  • Retention and deletion policy
  • Cross-border and vendor treatment rules

Leading practice

  • Attribute-based data authorization
  • Automated masking and tokenization
  • Purpose-aware data access
  • Continuous sensitive-data discovery
Deliverables: AI data classification standard, approved treatment matrix, source register, retention rules and privacy impact triggers.
🛡️
Stage 05

Define Security & Infrastructure

How will the organization isolate environments and protect identities, data and models?

Define identity, encryption, network, secrets, tenant isolation, logging, monitoring, resilience and incident requirements.

Minimum controls

  • Authenticated access and least privilege
  • Encryption in transit and at rest
  • Separated development and production
  • Central logging and incident contact

Enterprise controls

  • RBAC/ABAC and service identities
  • Private networking and approved endpoints
  • Key management and secret rotation
  • HA, DR, monitoring and security operations

Leading practice

  • Zero-trust AI gateway
  • Continuous posture assessment
  • Automated containment and kill switch
  • Sovereign and private model options
Deliverables: target security architecture, environment model, identity pattern, monitoring baseline, resilience requirements and incident playbook.
🔎
Stage 06

Select & Qualify AI Products

Which platform, model or vendor genuinely fits the business and control requirements?

Evaluate value, capability, integration, security, privacy, contractual terms, cost, portability and exit.

Minimum controls

  • Business-fit evaluation
  • Security and privacy questionnaire
  • No-training and retention review
  • Named vendor owner

Enterprise controls

  • Approved product and model registry
  • Architecture and legal review
  • Subprocessor and residency assessment
  • Exit, portability and continuity plan

Leading practice

  • Continuous vendor assurance
  • AI bill of materials
  • Multi-model routing and fallback
  • Independent control validation
Deliverables: product scorecard, vendor risk assessment, contractual control schedule, approved-use conditions and exit plan.
🏛️
Stage 07

Establish the Operating Model

Who makes decisions, enables delivery and supports production?

Define executive, governance, product, architecture, delivery, operations, assurance and support layers.

Minimum controls

  • Executive sponsor
  • Business and AI product owners
  • Named technology and risk contacts
  • Basic intake and approval forum

Enterprise controls

  • AI steering committee
  • Federated AI CoE
  • Product-based funding and support
  • Architecture, risk and operations forums

Leading practice

  • Enterprise AI platform organization
  • Reusable shared services
  • Continuous assurance capability
  • Outcome-based governance
Deliverables: target operating model, forum mandates, intake process, support model, funding model and escalation structure.
🤝
Stage 08

Define RACI & Stakeholder Acceptance

Does everyone understand their responsibility and decision authority?

Clarify accountability across business, technology, data, security, legal, privacy, compliance, operations and users.

Minimum controls

  • RACI for design, approval and operation
  • Named escalation owner
  • Stakeholder acceptance record
  • Decision log

Enterprise controls

  • Decision-rights matrix
  • Working-group mandate
  • Steering committee cadence
  • Formal risk and production sign-offs

Leading practice

  • Dynamic accountability by risk tier
  • Automated approval routing
  • Evidence-linked decisions
  • Continuous role recertification
Deliverables: RACI, decision-rights matrix, stakeholder acceptance, working-group charter and escalation path.
🏗️
Stage 09

Build the AI Foundation

Are the reusable controls, services and evidence pipelines ready?

Establish identity, model access, prompt governance, document controls, RAG, MCP governance, evaluation, monitoring and evidence.

Minimum controls

  • Approved model access
  • Prompt and data guardrails
  • Document and source controls
  • Basic evaluation and audit logging

Enterprise controls

  • AI gateway and scenario registry
  • RAG and embedding lifecycle
  • MCP/tool registry
  • Automated testing and release gates

Leading practice

  • Policy-driven control plane
  • Continuous red-team testing
  • Dynamic routing and containment
  • Immutable evidence vault
Deliverables: AI platform foundation, approved patterns, control catalogue, evaluation suite, evidence model and operational runbooks.
🧪
Stage 10

Implement Controlled Pilots

Does the capability improve work safely and measurably?

Pilot bounded use cases with representative users, clear success criteria, documented controls and fallback.

Minimum controls

  • Low-risk scope
  • Named user group
  • Manual fallback
  • Outcome and defect measures

Enterprise controls

  • Representative test scenarios
  • Security and privacy validation
  • Adoption and support plan
  • Go/no-go criteria

Leading practice

  • A/B evaluation
  • Automated red-team and regression tests
  • Human-factor measurement
  • Continuous experiment governance
Deliverables: pilot charter, test evidence, user feedback, value results, risk findings, remediation plan and production recommendation.
🚦
Stage 11

Move to Production

Are the business, technology, controls and people ready?

Complete architecture, security, privacy, legal, business, operational and change-management gates.

Minimum controls

  • Business owner approval
  • Security and privacy review
  • Production support and rollback
  • User communication and training

Enterprise controls

  • Evidence-based release gate
  • Operational readiness review
  • Risk acceptance and monitoring plan
  • Access and model configuration baseline

Leading practice

  • Automated evidence gate
  • Blue/green model and knowledge release
  • Real-time rollback
  • Continuous production assurance
Deliverables: production approval, release evidence, risk acceptance, support model, monitoring thresholds, rollback plan and user readiness confirmation.
📡
Stage 12

Monitor, Manage & Improve

Is the AI still safe, useful, compliant and cost-effective?

Monitor quality, value, drift, access, cost, incidents, vendor changes, SOP changes and user feedback.

Minimum controls

  • Usage and quality monitoring
  • Incident route
  • Periodic access review
  • Human feedback channel

Enterprise controls

  • Model, prompt, RAG and SOP change control
  • Drift and anomaly monitoring
  • Periodic recertification
  • Benefits and cost review

Leading practice

  • Continuous control testing
  • Automated affected-agent analysis
  • Dynamic thresholds and containment
  • Decision reconstruction at runtime
Deliverables: monitoring dashboard, incident register, change evidence, recertification record, improvement backlog and benefits report.
📈
Stage 13

Scale & Industrialize

How do we expand adoption without weakening control or trust?

Standardize reusable patterns, shared services, governance automation, workforce capability and portfolio management.

Minimum controls

  • Reusable delivery checklist
  • Approved patterns
  • Common training
  • Central use-case inventory

Enterprise controls

  • Shared AI platform and control plane
  • Federated delivery standards
  • Enterprise portfolio governance
  • Reusable evidence and evaluation assets

Leading practice

  • AI-native operating model
  • Policy automation
  • Continuous optimization
  • Enterprise learning and innovation ecosystem
Deliverables: scale roadmap, reference architectures, reusable controls, shared platform services, workforce plan and continuous-improvement model.
