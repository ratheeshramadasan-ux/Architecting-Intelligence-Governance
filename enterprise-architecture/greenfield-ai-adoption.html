Infrastructure

Cloud, network, identity, integration, data and operational requirements.

AI should progress from assisting people, to advising them, and only then to acting—when the organization is ready.As authority, sensitivity and business impact increase, human oversight, security, evidence and approval requirements also increase.

Assist

AI drafts, summarizes, searches or recommends. A person remains fully responsible for the outcome.

Advise

AI proposes an assessment or decision. A qualified person validates it before use.

Act

AI performs an action in a system. Permissions, limits, monitoring and intervention are explicitly controlled.

Industry standards and adoption

From recognized guidance to implementation-level detail

The framework does not replace industry standards. It brings their essential management, risk, security and regulatory principles together and translates them into a practical adoption sequence.

Standards contribute

  • ISO/IEC 42001: AI management-system requirements and continual improvement
  • NIST AI RMF: Govern, Map, Measure and Manage risk outcomes
  • ISO/IEC 23894: AI-specific risk-management guidance
  • ISO/IEC 27001 and enterprise security practices
  • Applicable regulatory and sector requirements, including risk-based obligations
Translate & OperationalizePrinciples become organizational decisions. Decisions become controls. Controls produce evidence.

Our framework implements

  • Step-by-step adoption sequence
  • Named governance forums and mandates
  • Lifecycle RACI and decision rights
  • Stakeholder communication and acceptance gates
  • Product qualification and architecture criteria
  • Control evidence, monitoring and escalation

Adoption levels

Start safely, operate consistently and scale with confidence

Minimum Viable Governance

Named sponsor and business owner, permitted-use boundaries, preliminary RACI, approved environment, basic security and privacy review, human review, logging, incident contact and pilot acceptance.

Standard Enterprise Implementation

Formal operating model, governance forums, lifecycle RACI, risk tiers, product qualification, environment separation, testing, monitoring, communication plan, acceptance gates and residual-risk workflow.

Leading-Practice Implementation

Integrated AI management system, runtime policy enforcement, continuous evaluation, end-to-end lineage, advanced testing, automated containment, independent assurance and portfolio-level optimization.

The appropriate level depends on industry obligations, data sensitivity, business impact, AI autonomy and intended scale. Not every organization needs every advanced capability on day one.
