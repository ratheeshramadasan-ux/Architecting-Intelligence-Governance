01 · Adoption journey

Build trust and capability in deliberate stages

The framework begins with business direction and readiness, establishes governance and technology foundations, then advances through controlled pilots, production and enterprise scale.

PHASE 1

Define & Assess

Direction, current state, maturity, risk appetite, data and stakeholder readiness.

PHASE 2

Govern & Design

AI type, risk tier, operating model, RACI, product qualification and architecture.

PHASE 3

Build & Prove

Foundation services, controls, low-risk pilots, evidence and user acceptance.

PHASE 4

Operate & Scale

Production gates, monitoring, dynamic change, industrialization and retirement.

02 · Classify AI and risk

Different AI types require different adoption paths

Assistive AI

Rules, document AI, summarization and low-risk copilots can be early-stage use cases with human validation.

Decision AI

Predictive and decision-support systems require data quality, explainability, outcome testing and recourse.

Action AI

Agents, MCP-connected tools and multi-agent systems require identity, tool authorization, action boundaries, approval and kill switches.

AI type × data sensitivity × business impact × autonomy × exposure × reversibility

03 · Governance model

Connect executive authority to delivery and runtime control

Executive Layer

Sets ambition, funds the portfolio, defines risk appetite and accepts material residual risk.

Governance Layer

Classifies use cases, applies policy, reviews architecture, privacy, security and Responsible AI evidence.

Delivery & Operations

Builds, tests, releases, monitors, supports and retires AI capabilities within approved boundaries.

05 · Technology and control foundation

Make governance executable

Identity, Data & Documents

Least privilege, data classification, DLP, source access, PII masking, retention, lineage and document controls.

Models, RAG & Vendors

Approved registries, routing, grounding, citations, residency, no-training clauses, versioning and fallback.

Agents, MCP & Evidence

Tool registry, identity propagation, schema validation, action limits, human approval, audit and rollback.

06 · Pilot to production

Prove value and control effectiveness together

Controlled Pilot

Bounded users, approved data, representative scenarios, human oversight and measurable outcomes.

Evidence Gate

Functional, security, privacy, adversarial, performance, retrieval, rollback and operating evidence.

Production Acceptance

Business, governance, architecture, security, legal, privacy and operations sign-off.

07 · Operate, change and scale

Govern the changing environment—not only the initial release

Continuous Monitoring

Value, quality, safety, drift, access, cost, incidents, user feedback and control effectiveness.

Dynamic SOP Change

Effective dates, affected-agent analysis, re-indexing, re-embedding, regression testing and controlled activation.

Scale and Retirement

Reusable patterns, shared platforms, recertification, vendor exit, identity revocation and evidence retention.

Fail-safe principle: no confirmed and applicable SOP means no autonomous execution. The agent stops and escalates rather than guessing.
