Architecting Intelligence · Guided Reading Path

From AI ambition to governed production operation

AI Governance and Enterprise Architecture are separate disciplines, but they should be read as one connected implementation journey. Governance defines what must be controlled. Architecture defines how those controls are implemented. Assurance proves they continue to work.

Recommended sequence: Adopt → Classify → Govern → Architect → Validate → Operate
Sequential journey

Six connected stages

Start with business readiness, determine what type of AI is being introduced, assign accountability, convert requirements into architecture, verify the evidence and operate the capability through continuous change.

01
AI Adoption

Start with organizational readiness

What are we trying to improve, and are we ready?

Define the vision, understand the current environment, identify stakeholders, establish risk appetite and build the minimum foundation before selecting complex AI technology.

Direction

Business outcomes, executive sponsorship and adoption principles.

Readiness

Process, data, technology, security and workforce maturity.

Progression

Controlled pilots, production gates and scaling criteria.

Read: Greenfield AI Adoption & Governance Framework →
02
AI Classification

Determine what is being adopted

What type of AI is this, and what could go wrong?

Classify Generative AI, predictive ML, document AI, RAG, decision support, agentic AI, MCP-connected agents and multi-agent systems using a consistent risk method.

Combined risk decision

AI type × data sensitivity × business impact × autonomy × external exposure × reversibility.

Change and assurance

Dynamic SOPs, MCP/tool changes, evidence reconstruction, supply chain and retirement.

Read: AI Classification, Change & Assurance →
03
AI Governance

Define accountability and decision rights

Who can approve, stop, challenge or accept the risk?

Establish executive authority, lifecycle governance, RACI, regulatory mapping, affected-person rights, acceptable use, risk acceptance and evidence requirements.

Governance & Compliance

Authority, policies, lifecycle gates, system register and regulation.

Data & Responsible AI

Privacy, access, sovereignty, human impact and transparency.

Agentic Governance

Autonomy tiers, tools, MCP, HITL, kill switch and board oversight.

Recommended next: translate the approved governance requirements into architecture controls.
04
Architecture & Implementation

Make governance executable

How will the required controls work in the actual solution?

Design identity, data, model, prompt, RAG, MCP, integration, evidence, monitoring and human-approval controls as part of the enterprise AI control plane.

Business & Operating

Capabilities, ownership, support model and governance forums.

Data & Security

Classification, lineage, access, masking, encryption and DLP.

AI Runtime

Model routing, prompts, RAG, tools, policy gates and evidence.

Read: AI Architecture & Implementation Framework →
05
Validation & Assurance

Verify before production

Can the organization prove the design is safe and ready?

Use an evidence-based security and architecture review covering governance, data flows, privacy, vendor risk, identity, model security, monitoring, resilience and operational readiness.

Security & Architecture Review

Blockers, required findings, evidence and approval conditions.

Production gate

Business, security, technology, legal, privacy and operational acceptance.

Recommended next: operate the approved capability with continuous monitoring, change control and incident readiness.
06
Operations & Continuous Assurance

Keep the capability trustworthy as it changes

Is the AI still behaving as intended?

Monitor performance, risk, cost, incidents, drift, changing SOPs, models, prompts, embeddings, MCP servers, tools and approval thresholds throughout the operational lifecycle.

Operational Risk

Behaviour, drift, incidents, containment, recovery and value.

Change Assurance

Dynamic SOPs, dependency impact, regression tests and evidence.

Retirement

Disable agents, revoke identities and tools, remove embeddings and verify exit.

One connected model

Adoption establishes capability. Classification determines proportionate controls. Governance defines accountability. Architecture makes controls executable. Validation confirms readiness. Operations continuously proves the controls remain effective.
